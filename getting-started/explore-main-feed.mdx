Once you sign up, you'll be taken to the **home page** where you'll see your main feed.

## Default Tracking Settings

When you first sign up, Neptureon automatically sets you up with **default tracking settings**. These are accounts considered valuable and useful today, so you'll start seeing posts right away.

## Your Main Feed

The main feed displays real-time posts from all the accounts you're tracking across every platform. This is where you'll spend most of your time, staying up to date with the latest updates and opportunities.

## What You'll See

Neptureon tracks **posts from all social media platforms**. Beyond just posts, Twitter tracking includes:

**Twitter Tracking:**
- Posts, replies, retweets, and quote tweets
- Follow and unfollow events
- Profile picture changes
- Pinned post changes
- Website/URL changes
- Bio/description updates
- Banner picture changes

This comprehensive tracking ensures you never miss any important activity or changes from the accounts you're monitoring.

## Understanding Posts

Posts are designed to be easily digestible. Each post displays:

- **Profile picture** of the source (who posted or published)
- **Display name** of the poster
- **Verification badge** (if the poster is verified on their platform)
- **Username or website** of the poster
- **Follower count** of the account
- **Time posted** (how long ago this event occurred)
- **Link to the original post** (direct link to view on the platform)
- **Post type indicators** (reply, retweet, quote, like, or regular post)
- **Media type indicators** (text only, image, video, link, carousel)
- **Source indicators** (which platform it came from)

Symbols are used instead of text to keep posts clean and easy to scan without cluttering them with labels.

### Truncated Content

Long captions are truncated to save space. For example, publications often include entire articles. Hover over any post to see the full caption.

Carousel media with more than 4 items is also truncated. Hover over the post to view all media in the carousel.

## Feed Settings

In the top left corner, click the gear icon to open feed settings:

### Post Order

Choose how posts are displayed:

- **Live Order (Default & Recommended)** - Posts appear as received from the platform. This is recommended because different platforms have different delivery speeds. For example, X posts arrive in milliseconds while Instagram posts may have a 15-second delay. Live order keeps the newest posts at the top regardless of platform delays.

- **Chronological Order** - Posts appear based on when they were originally posted on the platform. This can cause posts to appear jumbled since platform delays vary, making it harder to track new content.

### Animations

Toggle animations on or off for when new posts arrive in the feed.

## Feed Controls

### Pause/Play Feed

Next to the settings icon, use the pause/play button to control the feed:

- **Pause** - Click to stop new posts from appearing. This allows you to view current posts without movement or interruption.
- **Play** - Click again to resume the live feed.

**Auto-Pause:** Hovering your cursor over the feed automatically activates pause mode. The feed resumes when you move your cursor away.

### Search Feed

Use the search bar to filter posts. Type any text to see only posts that contain your search term. This filters through all visible posts in the feed.

### Platform Filters

Next to the search bar are 10 buttons, each representing a different social media platform. These control which platforms you receive posts from in your feed.

- **All Active by Default** - All platforms are enabled, so you'll receive posts from every source
- **Deactivate** - Click a platform's logo to stop receiving posts from that source
- **Reactivate** - Click again to resume receiving posts from that platform

This allows you to focus on specific platforms when needed.
